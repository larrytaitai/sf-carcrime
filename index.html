San Francisco has experienced a wave of property crimes in the past few years. In this data visualization story, we explore one particular kind of property crimes, car burglary, using the San Francisco Police Department crime incidence report data from 2003 to 2014. Property crimes are defined as arson, burglary, larceny/theft and vehicle theft. In this story, property crime incidents are re-grouped into two categories: car burglary and anything else. Overall, the city's car burglary crime incidents rise in proportion with other property crime incidents. But in different neighborhoods, the trend of car burglary varies dramatically.

In the South of Market Street neighborhood, both car burglary and other property crimes have surged since 2011. But car burglary has been increasing much more quickly than other property crimes. They nearly converged in 2014. South of Market Street is known to be a rough neigbhorhood.

In Noe Valley neighborhood, the number of car burglary and other property crimes remained more or less the same in the past very years. In fact, car burglary decreased a little from 156 in 2010 to 112 in 2014. Noe Valley is known to be a wealthier neigbhorhood.
